Since flat size details have not been shared, we can only make floor-level estimates to understand the special levies charged in 2019 and 2024.

Assuming that each flat was charged equal levies totalling approximately 1.5 lakhs per flat:

- 4 lower floors: ~ Rs. 10.5 lakhs per floor (7 flats x 1.5 lakhs = 10.5 lakhs)

- penthouse floor: ~ Rs. 3 lakhs (2 flats x 1.5 lakhs = 3 lakhs)

Difference ~ Rs. 7.5 lakhs.

This ~ 7.5 lakhs appears to be an imbalance in cost sharing between lower floors and top floor.

If the disproportionate monthly fee allocation I referred to yesterday was in effect from 2019, then, 7 years x Rs.66,000/year ~ over 4.6 lakhs.

So the total cross-subsidy from 28 lower flats to top two flats, since 2019 seems to be ~ 12 lakhs (~ 7.5 lakhs special levy + 4.6 lakhs from monthly fees).

If this method was prevalent prior to 2019, then the lower 28 could have paid even more than fair share.

But even just considering 2019 onwards, 12 lakhs is a substantial sum, by itself comparable to a special levy imposed on all of us. Several worthwhile projects could enhance our building with this kind of money.

Could the head board please confirm my calculations are roughly correct and clarify the basis for following the equal-per-flat method rather than a proportional floor-area model?

I'm happy to be corrected if any inaccuracies.
