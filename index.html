Regarding the tile project: In the 2024 gathering, the reason given for the Tile Project was to reduce flooding in the cellar during rains. The proposed solution (a proposer was not identified at that time) was to raise the cellar floor height by installing tiles.

I had raised the following points:

1. Increasing the cellar floor height alone would make no difference for a given amount of rain. For example, if there were 2 inches of flooding on the concrete floor, installing tiles that raised the floor height by—say—4 inches would still result in the same 2 inches of water now standing on top of the tiled surface.

2. Laying tiles requires significant preparation and skill. For example, if the concrete surface is not leveled beforehand, or if the tiles are not properly bonded during installation, they can crack. We knew in 2024 that this had already happened in a neighbouring building's cellar.

These basic questions were not addressed in 2024 but may have been discussed later in the group.

I request the proposer of the tile project to respond to the two points above. Also:

- What was the cost estimate? None was mentioned in the gathering.

- Please clarify the plan when tiles crack and the related expenses expected.
